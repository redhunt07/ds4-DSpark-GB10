# MTP / spec-decode paper findings — reproduction-grade (2026-06-02)

Full extractions from 6 PDFs (in `/tmp/mtp_papers/`, arXiv links inline) against
our setup: DeepSeek-V4-Flash MTP head, frozen base + frozen Q4_K experts, train
only the ~167M conditioning path, draft-2/k2 focus. Companion to `LEARNINGS.md`.

**The one-sentence finding:** our negative result is the textbook corpus-supervision
failure (incoherent multi-token joints), and FastMTP's *proven* recipe — which we
did NOT run — uses **self-generated data**; on top of that, **LK Losses** gives a
strictly better objective tested on DeepSeek-MTP heads, and **harvesting through
the quantized path** folds quant in for free.

## 1. FastMTP (2509.18362) — the proven base recipe we skipped

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.18362). MiMo-7B dense base, head-only (210.8M,
<3% of backbone), frozen base+embed+output. **Their own ablation: corpus
("Fixed-data FT") 1.67× vs self-gen ("Self-data FT") 1.81× — corpus is the weaker
arm. OUR negative result = their Fixed-data arm.**

- **DATA (§2.4):** main model **generates** the response ŷₙ for each instruction
  prompt (keep prompt, discard the source's response). 389.4K samples, EN+ZH,
  general/math/code. **Offline/precomputed**, 3 epochs. Generation temp unspecified
  → default **greedy** (matches their lossless framing).
- **LOSS (Eq 1-2):** hard-label **CE** (not KL) of step-k prediction vs the
  self-distilled shifted token. Weights `α_k = β^{k-1}/Σ_j β^{j-1}`, **β=0.6, K=3**
  → α = **[0.510, 0.306, 0.184]**.
- **RECURSION (§2.1):** single shared head. Step 1: base hidden hᵢ + embed(t_{i+1})
  → predict t_{i+2}. Step k>1: head's OWN output hidden h^{k-1} (EAGLE-style chain)
  + embed(t_{i+k}) → predict t_{i+k+1}. **Token inputs teacher-forced during
  training**; hidden state is what recurses. i ∈ [1, T−K]. **(Our unroll already
  does this — matches.)**
- **HYPERPARAMS (§3.1):** 3 epochs, cosine, peak LR **5e-5**, warmup 0.05, AdamW
  (0.9,0.95), global batch 64. <1 day on one H20.
- **RESULTS (§3.4):** per-step accept **70→81% (k1), 11→56% (k2), 2→36% (k3)**.
  2.03× speedup. K-sweep: accept-length rises monotonically with K but **speed
  peaks at K=3** → K=3 recommended.
- **EVAL (§3.1):** greedy (T=0), strict acceptance (lossless), Spec-Bench 7 tasks,
  SGLang, A10.
- **For us:** the recipe is otherwise correct; the bug was **data (corpus not
  self-gen)** + we used K=4 not K=3. Appendix A (data mix) not in the PDF — pull
  from github.com/Tencent-BAC/FastMTP if we need the exact prompt composition.

## 2. LK Losses (2602.23881, ICML 2026, Nebius) — better objective, tested on DeepSeek-MTP

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23881). **Replaces CE/KL with an
acceptance-DIRECT loss.** Tested on **DeepSeek-V3 MTP heads** (our closest analogue):
**+5.6% over KL** (which was already a big jump over the original head). Zero
inference overhead. Drop-in. Biggest gains in **low-capacity / high
draft-target-discrepancy** = exactly our frozen-Q4_K-expert regime.

- **Core identity:** acceptance `α = Σ_x min(p_x, q_x) = 1 − TV(p,q)` (p=target
  dist, q=draft dist). Maximizing accept ≡ minimizing TV. KL is only a proxy and
  **diverges from acceptance under capacity limits** (their §3.2 proof; toy
  Gaussian: TV 60.2% vs fwd-KL 50.2%).
- **Recommended loss — hybrid `L^λ_LK` (Eq 4-5):**
  ```
  L^λ_LK = λ·KL(p‖q) + (1−λ)·TV(p,q)
  λ = exp(−η · sg[α]),  η=3 (EAGLE-3 default)
  ```
  α aggregated over batch+seq, **per draft-position**. Low α (weak head) → λ→1 →
  KL-dominated (strong smooth signal); high α → λ→0 → TV-dominated (directly
  optimizes accept). **This automatically gives the weak k2 head stronger KL
  guidance — our k2-focus need, for free, per-position.** (Constant λ tested,
  inferior — the curriculum is necessary.)
- **Simpler alt `L^α_LK = −log Σ min(p,q)`**; gradient = `(1/α)·∇TV` — TV
  direction, KL magnitude, automatic scaling. `1/α` blows up as α→0 (intended;
  watch numerically at init — hybrid sidesteps it).
- **Multi-head aggregation:** weight head n by `γ^{n-1}`, **γ=0.8** (MEDUSA/EAGLE).
- **DATA/TRAIN (§5.3):** 660K Infinity-Instruct prompts, **responses
  self-generated by the target model** (same point as FastMTP), seq 8K, batch 64,
  LR **4e-4**, cosine, 100 warmup, AdamW(0.9,0.95), grad-clip 0.5, **T=1**.
  **DeepSeek-MTP: init from original MTP weights, fine-tune 1 EPOCH** (exactly our
  path).
- **Handles truncated vocab natively** (min(p,q) zeroes out-of-vocab); KL needs
  hacks there.
- **For us:** swap β-weighted CE → `L^λ_LK` (η=3, γ=0.8). 1-epoch FT on existing
  MTP weights. The per-position adaptive λ is the principled k2-focus.

## 3. MTP self-distillation (2602.06019, UMD/LLNL) — the pathology + on-policy nuance

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06019). **Diagnoses our exact negative result.**

- **THE PATHOLOGY (§2, zookeeper):** corpus CE supervises **marginals**
  P(x_{i+1}), P(x_{i+2}) independently. Optimal marginals **factorize into an
  incoherent joint** — "feed the panda bamboo" + "feed the lion meat" → sampling
  both independently gives "panda meat" half the time. The loss can't see
  cross-token correlation → recursive drafts derail → rejected. **k-dependent:
  ~comparable to online up to k≈2, then a 20% gap by k=5** (online 41.5% vs
  GT-supervised 19.0% at k=5; close at k=2: 60.7 vs 59.3).
- **ONLINE objective (Eq 2-3):** student argmax-rolls-out k tokens (on-policy
  student-forcing); frozen teacher (same init) chain-rule scores that joint
  `P_T(y'|x)=∏_j P_T(y'_j | y'_{1:j-1}⊕x)`; loss = CE of student-joint vs
  teacher-joint. **Hard teacher (argmax) is their main setting.**
- **On-policy is load-bearing:** even *offline teacher-generated* targets
  underperform online student-forcing at high k — the student must be critiqued on
  ITS OWN rollouts. **BUT this only matters at high k; offline self-gen is fine at
  k≤2.**
- **Scope:** they train the FULL model (all params); objective is
  architecture-agnostic and **can run head-only** (gradients into conditioning
  path only, frozen experts pass through). **Open risk = CAPACITY**: can a
  frozen-backbone head collapse joint entropy? Untested — instrument top-token
  confidence (their Fig 6 metric).
- **For us:** (a) confirms self-gen is the fix; (b) for **draft-2 (low k), offline
  self-gen (FastMTP) suffices** — we don't need online student-forcing yet; (c)
  flag: "if corpus failed even at small k, suspect an additional bug beyond
  joint-incoherence" — worth keeping in mind, but k2 IS where incoherence onsets.

## 4. MTP-D (2603.23911, Tencent) — KL-to-base term (subsumed by LK)

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23911). Closest config match (frozen base in the
looped stage, head-only, recursive single head).

- **Loss = CE + Top-N KL** where teacher = the base **main head's own next-token
  dist** (marginal), **Top-N=10000** (vocab 122880; Top-10k = 99.5% mass), **forward
  KL**, **stop-gradient on teacher logits**. Per-step: α_k=0.3 (CE) + **uniform β_k
  (1.0 @ K=1, 0.5 @ K=4)** — step-decay was tried and dropped.
- **Looped extension:** copy trained head-group weights to init the next group,
  freeze all prior, continued-pretrain only the new group. **Training-free 1→8 loop:
  distilled head holds CAR 26.7% @ head-3 vs 0.6% for CE-only** — predicts our k2
  jumps from the distillation term. +220% = 4→16 looped (3.204×) over 1-head.
- **For us:** the KL-to-base term is **largely subsumed by LK's hybrid** (LK's KL is
  KL(p_target‖q_draft) — the target IS the base dist). LK is the more principled
  version (acceptance-direct + adaptive λ). **Prefer LK; MTP-D confirms the
  direction.** One transferable detail if we hand-roll KL: Top-N=10000 + stop-grad,
  and since our base is frozen we can run β harder than they dared.
- Note: nothing here validated above ~10B — treat magnitudes as directional.

## 5. Attention Drift / "EAGLE 3.1" (2605.09992) — k2 norm fix, UNVALIDATED on MTP

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09992). [code](https://github.com/Dogacel/Attention-Drift).

- **Root cause:** unnormalized inter-step residual → hidden magnitude grows with
  depth (Llama-8B RMS 3.9→14.0 over 8 steps) → drafter acts like "stacked extra
  layers", collapses past training horizon.
- **Fix:** (a) **post-norm** — carry the RMSNorm'd hidden to the next step (not the
  raw residual); (b) **per-hidden-state RMSNorm** on each fused verifier input
  (h_low/h_mid/h_high) before the FC fusion. Post-norm/TTT-2 generalizes to depth-20
  where pre-norm collapses; cuts training-time-test 8→4.
- **THE MTP CAVEAT (§4.5, critical):** MTP heads **already use post-norm and STILL
  drift** (sharp onset + plateau, *different* profile, Fig 7/12). The norm fix is
  **NOT validated on MTP**; they flag **joint-pretraining-vs-posthoc** as an
  unquantified alternate cause. So the fix may be **necessary-but-insufficient** for
  an MTP head.
- **Requires retraining** (not a drop-in to an existing head).
- **For us — CONDITIONAL/lower-confidence:** (b) per-input RMSNorm + ensuring the
  inter-step recurrent carry is renormalized are in the conditioning path, low-risk
  — BUT **instrument our chain-h RMS vs depth FIRST** (replicate their Fig 12) to
  confirm magnitude growth is actually our k2 driver. The paper explicitly says it
  may not be, for MTP. Don't adopt blind.

## 6. ML-SpecQD (2503.13565, Intel) — quant of a self-aligned drafter is MINOR

[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.13565).

- **Intrinsic alignment = the SAME target weights direct-cast to 4-bit (no
  finetune)** → **71.2% accept (QA) / ~91% (code)** vs 41.7% for a 100×-smaller
  custom draft. WOQ perturbs but doesn't retrain → distribution stays aligned;
  custom drafts lose on capacity + alignment gaps.
- **Format: MXFP4 weights (32-elem blocks, E2M1 + E8M0 scale) + INT8 activations**
  — structurally ≈ our **Q4_K (256-elem superblocks) + Q8_K**. Q4_K is *higher*
  fidelity at 4-bit, so alignment should transfer or improve.
- **Verdict (medium confidence):** supports **skipping a standalone quant-aware
  realignment stage** — but the paper **trains nothing**, so it does NOT directly
  test harvest-through-quant vs QAT, and has **zero MoE data** (their models dense;
  our Q4_K is on the experts + discrete routing). Cleanest inheritance of their
  guarantee = **harvest the self-distill targets THROUGH the dequant-Q4_K/Q8_K
  forward** (our `enable_quant_aware` becomes the harvest fwd) so the trained head's
  target already includes quant. Validate with a cheap accept A/B before fully
  dropping QAT, given the MoE gap.

## SYNTHESIS — the literature-grounded next attempt (priority order)

The convergent recipe for OUR setup. Confidence in brackets.

1. **[HIGH] Self-generated data — the data bug.** Replace corpus with the base
   model's own greedy generations on a class-balanced (structured-list/chat-essay
   heavy) instruction-prompt set. This is FastMTP's *proven* recipe (11→56% k2) that
   we never ran. For draft-2 (low k), **offline self-gen suffices** (per
   2602.06019); online student-forcing is only needed if we push to high k later.
2. **[HIGH] LK acceptance-direct loss** (`L^λ_LK`, η=3, γ=0.8) replacing β-weighted
   CE. Tested on DeepSeek-MTP (+5.6% over KL), zero overhead, 1-epoch FT, and its
   per-position adaptive λ is the principled k2-focus. Subsumes MTP-D's KL term.
3. **[HIGH, free] Harvest through the quantized path** (Q4_K experts + Q8_K
   activations) so quant folds into the self-distill targets — our `enable_quant_aware`
   becomes the harvest forward. Per ML-SpecQD, this likely makes a *standalone* QAT
   run unnecessary.
4. **[CONDITIONAL] Attention-Drift norm fix** (per-input RMSNorm + inter-step
   renorm). **Instrument chain-h RMS vs depth first** — adopt only if our MTP head
   shows the magnitude-growth signature (the paper says it may not).
5. **[unchanged] Measure right:** draft-2 A/B in real ds4, accept-rate (not tps),
   greedy for matched denominators, vs the original head.

**The headline correction:** FastMTP got 11%→56% on k2 with self-gen data + β-CE.
We ran **corpus** and got nothing. Before any exotic lever, the single highest-value
move is **run FastMTP's actual recipe (self-gen data) + the LK loss.** That likely
recovers most of the gain we left on the table; the norm fix and standalone QAT are
secondary.

### Build implication for the harvest

The decode-path HC dump (~20 LOC, scouted) must run during **greedy generation**
(ds4 generates, we dump base-HC per generated position + the generated token),
**through the quantized expert path**. That single harvest tool unblocks items 1 + 3.
