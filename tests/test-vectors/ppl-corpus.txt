The river kept its own counsel. Each morning the surveyor walked the eastern bank
with a brass level and a notebook bound in oilcloth, marking the slow migration of
the channel against the iron pins his predecessor had driven a generation earlier.
The pins were rusted now, and two had vanished entirely beneath a gravel bar that
had not existed the previous spring. He recorded what he could measure and left the
rest to the patience of sediment, which answers every question eventually, though
never on the schedule of the men who ask it.

By noon the heat lay flat across the floodplain. Cattle stood in the shade of the
cottonwoods without moving, and the only sound was the dry tick of grasshoppers in
the fennel. The surveyor ate bread and a hard yellow cheese, drank from a canteen
that tasted faintly of tin, and considered the problem of the meander. A river that
bends will cut its outer bank and build its inner one, and so the bend grows sharper
until the neck pinches shut and the water abandons the loop altogether, leaving a
crescent lake to silt up and become, in time, a meadow. There was nothing to be done
about it. You could armor the bank with stone, and the river would simply find the
weakness you had not anticipated, because a river has more time than any engineer.

In the afternoon he climbed the bluff to triangulate from the old benchmark. From
the height the whole valley resolved into a single argument about gradient and load:
where the slope steepened the water ran clear and fast over cobbles; where it slackened
the channel braided and dropped its burden of silt; and at every transition the form
of the bed encoded the history of every flood that had ever passed. He thought that a
sufficiently patient reader could recover the climate of a century from the stratigraphy
of a cutbank, the way an astronomer recovers the chemistry of a star from a smear of
light. The thought pleased him, and he wrote it down, and then crossed it out, because
it was the kind of thing that sounded better than it was true.

He finished the traverse before the light failed and walked back along the levee. A
heron lifted from the shallows ahead of him, unfolding with the deliberate economy of
something that has never once in its life been in a hurry, and beat slowly downstream
until the dusk took it. The pins would still be there in the morning, or some of them
would, and the channel would have moved an imperceptible increment closer to the town,
and the calculation would begin again. This was the whole of the work: to measure a
thing that would not hold still, and to write the measurement down as though it would.

Consider now a small program that performs the same kind of bookkeeping against a
stream that does hold still, at least for the duration of a single pass. The function
below accumulates a running summary of a sequence of floating-point samples, tracking
the count, the mean, and the variance in a single traversal using Welford's method,
which is numerically stable in a way that the naive sum-of-squares is not.

def running_stats(samples):
    n = 0
    mean = 0.0
    m2 = 0.0
    for x in samples:
        n += 1
        delta = x - mean
        mean += delta / n
        delta2 = x - mean
        m2 += delta * delta2
    if n < 2:
        return (n, mean, float("nan"))
    variance = m2 / (n - 1)
    return (n, mean, variance)

def merge_stats(a, b):
    na, mean_a, var_a = a
    nb, mean_b, var_b = b
    n = na + nb
    if n == 0:
        return (0, 0.0, float("nan"))
    delta = mean_b - mean_a
    mean = mean_a + delta * (nb / n)
    m2a = var_a * (na - 1) if na > 1 else 0.0
    m2b = var_b * (nb - 1) if nb > 1 else 0.0
    m2 = m2a + m2b + delta * delta * (na * nb / n)
    variance = m2 / (n - 1) if n > 1 else float("nan")
    return (n, mean, variance)

The merge function is the part worth dwelling on. It exists so that the same summary
can be computed in parallel over disjoint partitions of the data and then combined
exactly, without revisiting any sample. The correction term, delta squared times the
product of the counts over their sum, accounts for the fact that the two partial means
were each computed against their own local origin; when the partitions are joined those
origins must be reconciled. Omit the term and the variance drifts in proportion to the
separation of the partition means, which is precisely the situation a careless test
will fail to exercise, because the obvious test feeds both halves the same distribution
and the means coincide and the bug stays hidden.

A regression suite, then, is an argument about which inputs are allowed to coincide. It
is not enough to show that the code produces the right answer on the inputs the author
imagined; it must produce the right answer on the inputs the author did not imagine,
and the only way to approach that asymptote is to keep adding cases until the failures
stop arriving. The river offers the same lesson on a longer clock. You armor the bank
you can see failing, and the next flood finds the bank you could not.
